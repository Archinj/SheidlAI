## 5. Objection Handlers

**"I already have Wordfence, why do I need this?"**
Wordfence is excellent at detecting threats — it's been protecting sites for years and has great threat intelligence. But detecting a problem and fixing it are two different things. When Wordfence tells you a plugin has a critical vulnerability, it's still on you to figure out what to do. ShieldAI picks up where Wordfence leaves off: it takes that detection, uses AI to generate the specific fix, and puts it in front of you for one-click approval. Think of ShieldAI as the execution layer Wordfence is missing.

**"I don't understand what the fixes mean — what if I approve something wrong?"**
That's exactly why we built the approval queue the way we did. Every fix comes with a plain-English explanation of the threat, a before/after diff showing exactly what will change on your site, and a confidence score from the AI. You don't need to understand PHP or server configuration — you just need to read one paragraph. And if you're still not sure, you can dismiss it and nothing changes. There's no wrong move.

**"What if the AI makes a mistake and breaks my site?"**
Before executing any approved fix, ShieldAI saves a snapshot of the current state — your .htaccess file, plugin version, or config setting as it exists right now. If anything goes wrong after the fix, you click Rollback and your site returns to exactly where it was. The approval queue means nothing happens until you say so, and rollback means you can always undo it. We've designed the whole system around the assumption that AI can be wrong — which is why a human always has to click Approve first.

**"It's too expensive — I already pay for Wordfence premium."**
The Shield plan is $79/year — $6.58/month. If your site gets hacked, the average cost to recover for a small business ranges from $500 to several thousand dollars in developer time, lost revenue, and reputation damage. ShieldAI doesn't just detect — it generates the fix and queues it for your approval, so threats get resolved instead of just logged. One prevented incident pays for years of ShieldAI. And there's a free tier you can start with to see the value before you commit.

**"I don't want AI touching my site."**
ShieldAI never touches your site unless you tell it to. The AI generates a recommendation — a specific, documented fix — and puts it in a queue. Nothing happens until you click Approve. If you click Dismiss, nothing happens either. The AI's job is to translate a security threat into a plain-English action item, not to make autonomous changes. You have complete control at every step, and every action is logged in the audit trail so you always know what happened and when.
